# Adobe Stock vs Shutterstock: Which Platform Should You Sell On?

Choosing the right stock photography platform can significantly impact your earnings. Adobe Stock and Shutterstock are the two dominant players, each with distinct advantages. Let's break down the key differences to help you make an informed decision—or better yet, succeed on both.

## Platform Overview

### Adobe Stock

Adobe Stock benefits from deep integration with the Adobe Creative Cloud ecosystem. Designers using Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign can search and license images without leaving their workflow.

**Key strengths:**
- Seamless Adobe Creative Cloud integration
- Growing buyer base (Adobe has 30M+ Creative Cloud subscribers)
- Higher commission rates for exclusive content
- Direct integration with Adobe Lightroom for submissions

### Shutterstock

Shutterstock is one of the largest and most established stock photography marketplaces. With a massive global buyer base, it offers high volume potential for contributors.

**Key strengths:**
- Enormous buyer base with global reach
- Enterprise clients and subscription plans
- Strong brand recognition
- Multiple content types (photos, vectors, videos, music)

## Commission Rates Compared

Understanding earnings structure is critical for contributors:

| Factor | Adobe Stock | Shutterstock |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Standard Rate | 33% of sale price | 15-40% (tier-based) |
| Exclusive Rate | Up to 35% | N/A |
| Earning tiers | Flat rate | Based on lifetime earnings |
| Minimum payout | $25 | $35 |

### How Earnings Work

**Adobe Stock** uses a straightforward model: you earn 33% of the sale price, regardless of volume. This simplicity is appealing to contributors at all levels.

**Shutterstock** uses a tiered system where your commission rate increases as your lifetime earnings grow:

```
Shutterstock Earnings Tiers:
Level 1: $0 - $100 lifetime → 15% commission
Level 2: $101 - $250 → 20% commission
Level 3: $251 - $500 → 25% commission
Level 4: $501 - $2,500 → 30% commission
Level 5: $2,501 - $25,000 → 35% commission
Level 6: $25,001+ → 40% commission
```

## Review Process

### Adobe Stock

- **Review time:** 3-7 business days on average
- **Acceptance rate:** Generally higher for quality content
- **Feedback:** Provides specific rejection reasons
- **Editorial content:** Accepted with proper model/property releases

### Shutterstock

- **Review time:** 1-5 business days (often faster)
- **Acceptance rate:** Moderate, with strict technical standards
- **Feedback:** Categorized rejection reasons
- **Editorial content:** Robust editorial section with news relevance

## The Multi-Platform Strategy

Here's the truth: **you don't have to choose just one**. Most successful stock photographers sell on both platforms simultaneously.

### Benefits of Multi-Platform Selling

1. **Maximum exposure** — Reach buyers on both platforms
2. **Diversified income** — Don't put all your eggs in one basket
3. **Different buyer types** — Adobe attracts designers; Shutterstock reaches marketers
4. **No exclusivity required** — Neither platform requires exclusive content

### The Challenge: Managing Metadata

The biggest pain point of multi-platform selling is managing metadata for each platform. Each has different:

- Title length requirements
- Keyword formats and limits
- Category systems
- Submission workflows

This is where uploading becomes exponentially more time-consuming.

## Recommendations

### For Beginners
Start with **Adobe Stock** for its simpler commission structure, then expand to Shutterstock once you have a workflow established.

### For Volume Contributors
**Both platforms** simultaneously, using automation tools to manage the metadata workload.

### For Niche Specialists
Focus on the platform where your niche performs best. Creative and design-oriented content tends to do better on Adobe Stock, while editorial and news content thrives on Shutterstock.
